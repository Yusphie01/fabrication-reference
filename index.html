üìñ EN Designation Format Explanation

‚Ä¢ S = Structural steel
‚Ä¢ 355 = Minimum yield strength (MPa)
‚Ä¢ J2 = Impact test 27J at -20¬∞C
‚Ä¢ +N = Normalized delivery condition

P265GH Example:
‚Ä¢ P = Pressure vessel steel
‚Ä¢ 265 = Minimum yield strength (MPa)
‚Ä¢ G = General use (suitable for boilers)
‚Ä¢ H = High temperature application

Impact Test Grades:
‚Ä¢ JR = 27J @ +20¬∞C (Room temp)
‚Ä¢ J0 = 27J @ 0¬∞C
‚Ä¢ J2 = 27J @ -20¬∞C
‚Ä¢ K2 = 40J @ -20¬∞C

NDT (Non-Destructive Testing) Methods

üîπ Visual Testing (VT)

Visual Inspection - ISO 17637 / ASME V Art.9

Coverage: 100% inspection, primary method

Equipment: Welding gauge, magnifier, ruler, mirror

Inspection Points:

  • Weld profile, reinforcement height
  • Undercut depth and length
  • Surface porosity, cracks
  • Overlap, incomplete fusion
  • Spatter, arc strikes
Primary Method100% Coverage

üîπ Radiographic Testing (RT)

RT - ASME Section V Article 2

Radiation Source: X-ray or Gamma ray (Ir-192, Co-60)

Film Classification: Class I, II, III (I = finest grain)

IQI (Penetrameter): Wire type or hole type

Detects: Porosity, inclusion, crack, lack of fusion/penetration

Sensitivity: 2% of thickness minimum

VolumetricPermanent Record

üîπ Ultrasonic Testing (UT)

UT - ASME Section V Article 4

Method: Pulse Echo technique

Probe Types: Straight beam (0¬∞), Angle beam (45¬∞, 60¬∞, 70¬∞)

Frequency: 2-5 MHz typical (lower for thick/coarse grain)

Calibration: V1, V2, Distance-Amplitude blocks

Detects: Lamination, crack, lack of fusion, porosity

VolumetricThickness >10mm

üîπ Magnetic Particle Testing (MT)

MT - ASME Section V Article 7

Material: Ferromagnetic only (carbon steel, low alloy)

Method: Wet fluorescent or Dry powder

Current: AC, DC, Half-wave DC (HWDC)

Technique: Continuous or Residual magnetization

Detects: Surface & near surface cracks (0-5mm depth)

Welding Defects & Quality Control

üîπ Common Welding Defects (ISO 6520)

Group 1: Cracks

Types:

  • Hot Crack: Solidification, centerline, crater crack
  • Cold Crack: Hydrogen-induced, toe crack, HAZ crack
  • Lamellar Tearing: Through-thickness crack in base metal

Causes: High restraint, hydrogen, improper WPS, fast cooling

Prevention: Preheat, low hydrogen electrode, PWHT, proper joint design

NOT ACCEPTABLECritical Defect

Group 2: Porosity

Types:

  • Uniform Porosity: Gas bubbles evenly distributed
  • Cluster Porosity: Localized concentration
  • Wormhole: Elongated tubular cavity
  • Piping Porosity: Vertical wormhole to surface

Prevention: Dry electrodes, clean surface, proper gas flow (GMAW)

Group 3: Solid Inclusions

Types:

  • Slag Inclusion: Trapped slag between passes
  • Tungsten Inclusion: From TIG electrode contamination
  • Oxide Inclusion: Metal oxide trapped in weld
  • Metallic Inclusion: Foreign metal particles

Prevention: Proper inter-pass cleaning, grinding, correct welding parameters

Group 4: Lack of Fusion & Penetration

Types:

  • Lack of Fusion (LOF): No fusion between weld and base metal or between passes
  • Lack of Penetration (LOP): Weld doesn't penetrate through joint thickness
  • Incomplete Root Penetration: Root not fused through

Causes: Low heat input, fast travel speed, wrong angle, contamination

Prevention: Proper current, correct technique, adequate root gap

NOT ACCEPTABLEStructural Risk

Group 5: Imperfect Shape & Dimension

Types:

  • Undercut: Groove at weld toe, reduces cross-section
  • Overlap: Weld metal flows over base without fusion
  • Excessive Reinforcement: Too much weld metal buildup
  • Excessive Penetration: Drop through, icicles
  • Concavity: Root or face depression
  • Burn Through: Hole melted through base metal

Group 6: Miscellaneous Defects

  • Arc Strike: Base metal damage from stray arc
  • Spatter: Metal particles on base metal surface
  • Distortion: Base metal warping from heat
  • Shrinkage: Weld metal volume reduction

üîπ Hydrogen Cracking Prevention (4 Factors)

The 4-Factor Model for Hydrogen Cracking:

1. HYDROGEN:
‚Ä¢ Use low hydrogen electrodes (E7018, E8018)
‚Ä¢ Store electrodes at 120-150¬∞C
‚Ä¢ Dry electrodes before use
‚Ä¢ Max exposure: 4 hours for low-H electrodes
‚Ä¢ Keep base metal dry and clean

2. HARDNESS (Susceptible Microstructure):
‚Ä¢ Control HAZ hardness < 350 HV (typical)
‚Ä¢ Preheat to slow cooling rate
‚Ä¢ Avoid martensite formation
‚Ä¢ PWHT to temper microstructure

3. STRESS:
‚Ä¢ Reduce restraint (joint design)
‚Ä¢ Control residual stress
‚Ä¢ Proper fit-up and tack welding
‚Ä¢ Balanced welding sequence

4. TIME (Diffusion):
‚Ä¢ Delay between welding and cooling
‚Ä¢ Maintain interpass temperature
‚Ä¢ Slow cooling after welding
‚Ä¢ Hold time before final cooling

üìä Carbon Equivalent (CE) Formulas

IIW Formula (Most Common):
CE = C + Mn/6 + (Cr+Mo+V)/5 + (Ni+Cu)/15

Pcm Formula (for thin sections):
Pcm = C + Si/30 + (Mn+Cu+Cr)/20 + Ni/60 + Mo/15 + V/10 + 5B

CET Formula (for high heat input):
CET = C + (Mn+Mo)/10 + (Cr+Cu)/20 + Ni/40

Interpretation:
‚Ä¢ CE < 0.35% ‚Üí Excellent weldability, no preheat
‚Ä¢ CE 0.35-0.40% ‚Üí Good weldability, preheat for thick sections
‚Ä¢ CE 0.40-0.60% ‚Üí Fair weldability, preheat recommended
‚Ä¢ CE > 0.60% ‚Üí Poor weldability, preheat mandatory, special procedures
